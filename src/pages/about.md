# About Me

<img className="about-headshot" src="/img/zachary-cohen-headshot.jpg" alt="Portrait of Zac Cohen" />

I'm Zac Cohen, a systems-minded electromechanical engineer, maker, educator, and builder of practical engineering tools.

My work sits at the intersection of mechanical design, electronics, embedded systems, controls, fabrication, and documentation. I like projects where hardware, software, and people all have to work together — especially the messy middle where good systems thinking can turn a fragile prototype into something reliable, teachable, and maintainable.

Professionally, I've worked across advanced prototyping, robotics, electromechanical integration, PCB design, embedded systems, additive manufacturing, and technical documentation. I've supported research and development projects involving mechanical assemblies, control systems, sensor integration, fabrication workflows, and hardware bring-up. I also teach SolidWorks and engineering technology, which has strongly shaped how I document and explain technical work.

This site is a hub for the projects, guides, tools, and engineering resources I'm building. Some are professional portfolio pieces. Some are educational resources. Some are open-source tools. Some started because I saw a gap, got annoyed enough, and decided to build the thing I wished already existed.

A few themes show up often in my work:

- turning vague technical problems into clear system architectures
- designing hardware that is easier to build, test, troubleshoot, and explain
- creating documentation that helps real people make better engineering decisions
- using automation, embedded systems, and software to make physical systems smarter
- building educational tools that make engineering more approachable

I'm especially interested in electromechanical systems, robotics, embedded controls, connector and cable design, PCB workflows, additive manufacturing, smart-home automation, and engineering education.

The "nerd" part of the name is intentional. I like deep dives. I like clean diagrams. I like weird edge cases. I like when a project has both a spreadsheet and a soldering iron involved. Most of all, I like building useful things and then explaining them clearly enough that someone else can build on them.

This site is where all of that lives.
